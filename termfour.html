Work-Integrated Learning Experience at the Royal Canadian Mint

What is the Royal Canadian Mint?

The Royal Canadian Mint is a Crown corporation that produces all of Canada's circulation coins. It also manufactures circulation coins on behalf of other nations. The Mint also designs and manufactures precious and base metal collector coins, gold, silver, palladium, and platinum bullion coins, medals, and medallions. The Mint operates facilities in Ottawa, Winnipeg, and a sales office in Vancouver. The Mint is a for-profit organization and pays dividends to the Crown.

What did I do at the Royal Canadian Mint?

I was a part of the IT department at the Royal Canadian Mint. I was assigned to the IT Service Desk team and was responsible for providing technical support to the employees of the Mint. I was also responsible for the maintenance of the IT equipment and the inventory of the IT department. I was also assigned to a project that involved the implementation of a new software that would be used by the employees of the Mint. I was responsible for the testing of the software and the creation of the training material for the employees.

What did I learn at the Royal Canadian Mint?

  • How to provide technical support in an enterprise setting.
  • How to troubleshoot issues with the legacy equipment and software.
  • How to manage the inventory of an IT department such as hardware, software, and licenses.
  • How to test software and create training material for future use of equipment or programs.
  • How to work in a professional environment and be a positive influence in any work environment.
